Lake-effect snow is a prominent occurrence in Michigan, when cold air passes over the warmer waters of the Great Lakes, picking up moisture which leads to intense snowfall when it reaches the colder land. This effect is particularly pronounced on the Eastern and Western shores of the state. Cities on the Eastern side and Western areas near Lake Michigan often witness increased snowfall due to this process. In southern Michigan, the snowfall is typically less extreme but still significant. Storm systems moving across the central part of the United States can bring widespread snowfall to the entire state. These systems, combined with the lake-effect snow in certain regions, contribute to the overall snowfall patterns. Most counties in Nebraska passed a season that had ten to twenty inches less than they were expected to receive. In conclusion, the 2021-2022 snowfall season fell far short of most expectations.
